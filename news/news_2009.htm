         The Philly Great American Vintage Guitar Show was a Incredible!
            My wife Teri and I attended the show on Saturday, November 9th and
            had a great time!  I didn't end up bringing any treasures home this year,
            but I saw a treasure room full!  There were probably 150 vendors with
            booths selling acoustic and electric guitars of every size, shape and  
            vintage!  Not much had changed in the three years since we have been  
            except the prices...just like everything else for sale in this economy
            they were down a bit.  Still I couldn't find anything under $4000 dollars
            that I would have wanted and that was too rich for my bank account
            this year! I did fall in love with a 3/4 size 1956 Gibson LG2, but it was not
            practical since it couldn't use it on stage and I have enough guitars sitting
            at home now as it is.  Teri and I also ran into Greg Hollenbach, whom I
            met through the Acoustic Guitar Magazine web-based Community, and
            we had a few minutes to exchange comments on the show! 
            A good time was had by all and I have the T-shirt to prove it!

  • Sparks, Raymond and McCoy are booked to play for ARF!

    The Animal Resource Foundation (ARF) has again this year, booked us to play for their annual fund-raiser crab feast at the Kent Island Yacht Club on August 23rd from 1 to 4pm.  Here is some info on the event from ARF...

    Here is what ARF does: 
    Animal Resource Foundation is dedicated to saving and improving the lives of abandoned, abused and neglected companion animals throughout the region.  Since its founding in 1999 ARF has helped thousands of cats and dogs from the danger of euthanasia.  Special medical services are provided for disabled and abused animals.  Each animal receives routine vaccinations, are spayed and neutered prior to adoption and special needs animals are rehabilitated.  Every animal that enters ARF is loved and cared for as if they were our own before they are adopted to carefully selected loving homes.

    Each membership, donation and proceeds from fundraisers the funds are used to help us further our efforts to spay, neuter, off-set medical expenses and to raise awareness to this very special cause.  It also keeps food in the animals bowls and helps maintain our adoption center while the animals are staying with us.

    From 1992 through 1999, I had a small home recording studio called “Pepperland Studio.”  During that time I recorded many songs on my own and with others.  I also recorded an album with The Free Flight Band, of which I was a member.  This is a collection of songs from that time.   The “solo” recordings are true solos, as I performed all of the vocals and instruments.  The Free Flight recordings are my compositions from our 1993 album “Uncharted Skies.”   The CD set is available at performances for $10.00.
