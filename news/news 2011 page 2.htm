Work continues on my 1980 Martin
D-28-12 string at Raymond Guitars...
At the shop today, we removed the neck with at "steam machine"
and a special brace.  Next step is to shave the neck joint to reset the
angle and then put it all back together, refret the neck, and it will be better than new!  I will post pictures of the progress here.

The Sparks Solid Body "Lily" Guitar  is making  progress...
 Work began on a new 6 string solid body electric guitar a few weeks ago and it is coming along!  A recent visit to Raymond Guitars yielded the mahogany body and curly maple top sanded, book matched and glued. 
The next step, hopefully this week, is to remove glue residue and sand the surfaces to ready for gluing the top to the back. The plan is now to name the guitar after my new Granddaughter "Lily" and inlay a 2011 penny into the tailpiece along with Lily's name in mother-of-pearl (see computer rendering below). I will continue to post progress!

The newest member of the
Sparks Guitar Collection is born...
 Work began on a new 6 string solid body electric gutiar with a visit
to my friend Patrick Raymond's shop, Raymond Guitars, by picking
out the rough billet of Mahogany for the body.  The rough billet,
which has "Ed's" written on it, will be book-matched and then "capped"
with a 1/2" piece of book-matched curly maple. The design is based on Turner's "Model One" body style, but will incorporate uniquely designed electronics and inlay. Check back as we will continue to
post pictures here of the "build" as it progresses!

Top: Ed with the neck and Billet
Bottom: Rendering of the body		 Top: 3.5" solid mahogany billet
Bottom: Headstock with new keys
