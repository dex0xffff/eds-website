  • The new arrival!  Raymond Guitars first 12 string...
    December 17, 2008
         My long time friend and bandmate, luthier Patrick Raymond of the newly formed Raymond Guitars, brought by a brand new Model RJ12 acoustic 12 string today for me to try out.  The sound, especially of the open low "E" strings was incredible!  The guitar was based on extensive measurements Patrick took from my Guild JF212Xl.  That would account for how natural it felt in my hands.  I played it for about 30 minutes, but then he made me give it back so he could deliver it to it's new owner.

  • INCREDIBLE NEWS!
    October 25, 2008
    They are back!  Literally minutes before going on stage for a benefit for the AA County Disabilities Office, my cell phone rang and it was Matt Bowers, the General Manager at the Guitar Center in Glen Burnie...a young man brought in a Hofner Beatle bass to sell for cash...long story short, Matt recognized it as my stolen bass and called the Police...about an hour later I had both my bass and my Guild 12 string!  

                        Ed with Matt Bowers the "Guitar Center" Hero!

    Many thanks to my friends and bandmates for their support, and all the stores who supported me by taking my information flyer in hopes that someone would come in with the guitars...Dale at The Severna Park Music & Arts Center, Rick at Garrett Park Guitars in Annapolis, Emory at Appalachian Bluegrass, Bruce at Bruce Sandler's Guitar Network, Jim's Guitars, and Brian at Bill's Music House, all in Catonsville.  Thanks to the Public Safety Office at AACC for getting the Police report out immediately!  And of course my greatest thanks to Chris Rush and Matt Bowers of the Glen Burnie Guitar Center...Chris for taking my missing instruments flyer in and quickly circulated them amongst the area Guitar Center Stores, and of course Matt, for recognizing my bass when it was brought in, and calling the Police.  Also, a big thank you to the Police for professionally handling the recovery of my beloved instruments!   They are both in perfect shape, and are now home resting, recuperating and readying themselves for the next show!

  • No news...
    October 16, 2008
    No word on the two stolen guitars.  I have ordered two brand new Guilds, one each of the only two remaining American-made Guild 12 strings, the F412 and F512. They are identical with the exception of the back and sides wood, maple or rosewood.  When they finally do come in I will choose the one I like best to purchase.  Once gain I have received a deal that I can't pass up from Brian Higgins of Bill's Music in Catonsville (link on the links page of my web site).  I will post pictures of the one I choose in the Guitar Gallery.  Many thanks to all of the kind words I have received about the loss of the two guitars.  

 

  • Bad news...
    October 4, 2008
    Two of my guitars were stolen recently after a show. The two instruments were my 1980 Guild JF212XLN 12 string acoustic and the second was my Hofner Icon Beatle "B" Bass.  A Police report was filed and we still hold out hope for their return. The Hofner bass was a gift from my wife and held great sentimental value and so it's heartbreaking to lose.  It took 6 months to get it once she ordered it and another 6 months to get the case! I will eventually replace it, but not just now as I have two other basses to use.  The second is my Guild 12 string.  This instrument was 28 years old (older than all of my children!) and I will be replacing it ASAP with a new Guild. Check the Guitar Gallery for pictures of the new 12 sting~!
