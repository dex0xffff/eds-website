Guitar Building NEWS...
 Yesterday I began two projects and continued a third working with Patrick Raymond of Raymond Guitars (also my band mate!)
I continued work on building my lap steel...
I have been working on this for a couple of years now as a side project...
of course once it's finished I have to learn to play it!

I also started two other projects...
I began a neck reset project on my 1980 Martin D-28-12 guitar. 
It is my hope to get this fixed once and for all and make it playable again,
and learn a lot from Patrick in the meantime!  As you can see in the picture,
I got so excited about getting the project started that I removed the 4th fret instead of the 15th fret as Patrick had instructed...but it's okay, they all get replaced before the job is finished!  The 15th fret gets removed so we can drill a hole for a small needle to be inserted into the neck joint to inject steam into the joint, melting the glue and allowing us to remove the neck for the badly needed "reset." 

Lastly, we started refurbishing an old neck I had built which I will use on a solid body electric I have had in my mind for quite some time.  The first step was to remove the old heel and then remove all of the frets to re-radius the fingerboard from flat to a 16" radius.  Here is a picture of a few of the steps:
