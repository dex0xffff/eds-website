Edward Sparks & Patrick Raymond
 

created the Red Door Coffeehouse after meeting and performing at other local coffeehouses. They ran the Red Door for almost 10 years before closing to pursue more stage performance time.  They have performed together at the 333 Coffeehouse in Annapolis, the Crofton City Limits Coffeehouse, Borders Bookstore in Bowie, the "Starbucks Circuit" in Maryland and Virginia, the Church Street Coffeehouse in Cambridge, Coffee Guru's Cafe in Annapolis, Barnes & Noble Bookstores in Annapolis and at the Inner Harbor in Baltimore, the Annapolis Children's Museum, at the Earth Day Celebration and Arts Festival at Quiet Waters Park, Queen Anne's County Arts Fest, Queen Anne's County Farmers Markets, Anne Arundel Community College, Fall Fest at Homestead Gardens/Annapolis, the Music-Go-Round music store, presented a folk-music program at Arlington Echo Music Camp, at Boy's Latin of Baltimore, local schools, and much more.
