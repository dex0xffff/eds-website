Guitar Gallery

 

Company: Harmony
Model#: Sovereign 1260
Year: 1971
Description: This instrument was purchased by my parents from Best Products in Baltimore Maryland.  The guitar is in good condition, with a minor 1/4" round finish flaw caused by a synthetic strap sticking to the finish.  There is also a small hole in the back from a botched output jack installation.  There is a "shadow" in the finish on the treble side of the bridge where an original Barcus Berry pickup was stuck for many years.  This guitar was my step up to a solid wood bodied guitar. It's purchase was inspired by my good friend Richard Campbell, who played the same model.  This same model was also played by John Sabastion at Woodstock, and a 12 string version was played by Pete Townsend of the WHO.   This guitar appeared in Vintage Guitar Magazine in a picture with my other three Harmony guitars called "Four Part Harmony."   All of my Harmonys were made in the USA, and with the exception of the Stella, are all solid wood instruments, and this one features an  adjustable steel rod in the neck.  Included in the thumbnails below are an original Sovereign ad from the 1970's, a shot of me performing with this guitar in about 1975. Yes that is a picture of Led Zeppelin with a Sovereign in 1971. John Sebastian of the Lovin' Spoonful used one at Woodstock.  Pete Townsend played this model as is evidenced by the picture below and also owned him with a 12 string version of this guitar.
