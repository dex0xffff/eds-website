Guitar Gallery

 

Company: Harmony
Model#: Sovereign Patrician Archtop
Year: 1955
Description: At the time I found this instrument I didn't own an archtop guitar at all.  I wandered into my friend's music store in Annapolis, Garrett Park Guitars, and spotted it on the rack.  Earlier, I had fell in love with an old Gretsch Syncromatic he had for sale, but lost interest when I realized that the previous owner had broken the neck and apparently glued it back on crooked! I love natural tops and this one looked radiant.  I noted that the headstock was identical to the on my Harmony Sovereign flat-top, the second guitar I owned. I took it down and strummed it and the sound was as great as the action on the neck!  When Rick Hogue, the owner, told me it was all solid wood and was made in 1955 I had to have it...I was born in 1955, so it was meant to be.  It is in excellent, all original, condition, but did not come with a case.  This guitar appeared in Vintage Guitar Magazine in a picture with my other three Harmony guitars called "Four Part Harmony."   All of my Harmonys were made in the USA, are all solid wood instruments,and this one features an adjustable steel rod in the neck.
