Guitar Gallery

 

Company: Teisco/Sparks
Model#: Woody
Year: 1968
Description: This is a Japanese-made bass was a gift from one of my best friends growing up, Kenny Sassaman.  This is another guitar I modified extensively.  It was originally red with plastic everything...pickguard, pickups, slide switches, even plastic "mother-of-toilet seat" inlays!  About the only part of it that remains are the neck, body, thumbrest, bridge, and tuning keys.  I fashioned a new pick- guard from maple, a walnut pickup surround, and a Dean Markley acoustic guitar soundhole pickup, which has since been removed. The neck is made of about 30 or 40 thin veneers of maple glued together vertically, an Asian trick to make a cheap, but rock strong neck.  It originally had fender style volume and tone knobs on it.  I also wired in a "dead" switch. Because I removed all of the finish and left it natural, I dubbed it "Woody."  When my son Josh began to show interest in the bass, I loaned it to him to learn on, and it certainly did the trick!
