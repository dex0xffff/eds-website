Guitar Gallery

 

Company: Yamaha
Model#: FG260
Year: 1975
Description: I acquired this guitar new, as a replacement for an Epiphone 12 string which was permanently damaged while being repaired by the Music House. The salesman told me to choose any guitar on their shelf, with the exception of a Martin 12 string valued at 4 times the price! My choices came down to two 12 string guitars in the damaged guitar's price range...an Ibanez, which was a new line at the time, and this Yamaha.  He told me that the shiny gloss finish on the Ibanez' light maple fingerboard would soon begin to wear off and the light wood underneath would show wear and dirt, like a Telecaster or Strat eventually does .  He said that this Yamaha guitar could be tuned to concert pitch, because of the massive neck, and with a little care  it would give me many years of playing time...and he was right!  The original tuning keys didn't hold tune very well and so I soon had them professionally replaced with a $100.00 Schaller "mini" set. The classical style slotted head made the guitar difficult to restring, but the new keys held their tune much better than the originals.  For amplification, this instrument was fitted with an original  Bill Lawrence soundhole pickup, with internal wiring and a side mounted jack, like my Gibson J160E.  This guitar was retired in 1980 when it was replaced by my new Guild FG212XL 12 string. I kept it until until 1994, when I sold it to raise funds to purchase a new Ovation Acoustic Bass.  When sold, the guitar was in very good condition, playable when tuned to concert pitch, and left with the original hardshell case.   This sturdy 12 string was a much loved guitar that saw many hours of stage time, from the mid 70's and through all of the 80's.
