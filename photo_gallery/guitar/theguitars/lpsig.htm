Guitar Gallery

 

Company: Gibson
Model#: Les Paul Signature
Year: 1976
Description: This instrument was purchased used from the owner. It is a rare bird these days as Gibson only produced a little more than 4000 of the model between 1975 and 1979, before it was discontinued, due to lack of public acceptance and sales. Guitarist did not like Low-imp pickups. The guitar has two outputs, one low-imp and one hi-imp. See the pictures of the body edge and the original ad below.  The finish came in sunburst, but this model has a gold top, similar to the solid LP gold tops.  Although the controls look like the typical setup, they are actually one volume and tone control, a phase switch, and a imp selector switch. This is the only Les Paul model with a semi-hollow body design. The guitar was in bad shape and needed extensive repairs and restoration, all done by myself...but then I only paid $300 for it! The original hardshell case was trashed, it even had what looked like a bullet hole in it.  I replaced it with a tan Epi case right away.  I also replaced the tuners, the switch ring, the bridge and tailpiece, and I made a cream pickguard to match the original. The plastic pickup covers had been defaced, but the low impedance pickups were in perfect working order.
