Company: Hofner
Model#: Beatle Bass Icon Series
Year: 2006
Description: This instrument was a gift from my wife for Christmas of 2006 and so it holds a great deal of sentimental value.  It was such a popular item that year that it was backordered from Musician's Friend, the vendor she purchased it from, for six months!  When it finally came in I went to Bill's Music House and ordered the Hofner hardshell case made for it, and that took another six months to arrive!  It is a great instrument and a nod to it's '60's cousin, made famous by Paul McCartney.            Though Asian made, unlike the other Hofner Beatle bass copies made by Epiphone, Jay Turser, and others, the Icon B-Bass is authorized by the Hofner Company in Germany.  It looks and sounds great, and has the Hofner name inlaid in the headstock in real mother-of-pearl, not a decal like the original.  Like the original, it has cheaper plastic keys, but they do consistently hold tune.  Also like the original, the controls are little hard to make sense of, but do work well in live applications. The "solo" button gives a nice volume boost.   The instrument is intonated with actual fret wire in parallel slots running across the archtop style bridge.  It has that "thump in the chest" bass sound when amplified.  As did Macca, I immediately removed the gaudy pearloid plastic pickguard.  Unlike many solid body basses, this bass is so small and lightweight it is a pleasure to play all night on a strap!    I owned this instrument for 2 years before it was stolen in September, 2008.  Unbelievably, the bass was returned to me 6 weeks later after the person who stole it tried to sell it back to the local Guitar Center!
