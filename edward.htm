Edward Sparks

Edward Sparks has been performing for the last 25+ years. He plays the 6 and 12 string acoustic and electric guitars, acoustic and electric bass, harmonica, and a "little banjo and mandolin."
 
Edward won a songwriting contest
sponsored by Acoustic Guitar Magazine.

He likes to collect instruments and owns a variety of instruments including a"banjolin," a very old hybrid instrument which has the neck of a mandolin attached to a small banjo body.

Edward has also built a few instruments, which are shown below:

Details of instruments built by Edward Sparks:
Top: 8 string tenor guitar Far right: head of 6 string semi-solid body mandolin
Bottom row: 12 string semihollow electric guitar
All bird inlays are ivory, made from key tops from an old piano, except
bird in the center given to Sparks by Paul Reed Smith, which is abalone.
Eagle on pickguard on top row is ivory, oak wings, and abalone.
Besides performing  with Patrick Raymond as "Sparks & Raymond," an acoustic duo, he performs with Steve McCoy and Joel McGuire as part of the acoustic/electric/electronic trio, called FREE FLIGHT, founded by Sparks some 15 years ago. Their regular set lists include "covers" from Beatles to Pink Floyd, Crosby- Stills-Nash & Young to Genesis, Cat Stevens to the Electric Light Orchestra, James Taylor to Gin Blossoms, with originals as well!
